About Kandy City

Kandy, nestled amidst the hills of the Central Province, is Sri Lanka's cultural heart and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It was the last capital of the ancient kings' era of Sri Lanka and holds immense historical and religious significance, primarily due to the Temple of the Sacred Tooth Relic.

The city is a vibrant blend of rich heritage, natural beauty, and spiritual sanctity. Key aspects that make Kandy a must-visit destination include:

  • Home to the sacred Sri Dalada Maligawa (Temple of the Tooth Relic), a vital pilgrimage site for Buddhists worldwide.
  • Surrounded by lush tea plantations, diverse rainforests, and the picturesque Kandy Lake.
  • Renowned for its vibrant cultural festivals, most notably the Esala Perahera, a grand annual procession.
  • Gateway to the stunning Royal Botanical Gardens, Peradeniya, showcasing a vast collection of flora.
  • Offers a cooler climate compared to the coastal regions, providing a refreshing escape.

Featured Activities

Visit Temple of the Tooth

Explore the sacred Sri Dalada Maligawa, housing the relic of the tooth of the Buddha, a spiritual and architectural marvel.

Explore Botanical Gardens

Wander through the magnificent Royal Botanical Gardens, Peradeniya, known for its vast collection of orchids and diverse plants.

Attend a Cultural Show

Experience traditional Kandyan dance and drumming performances, showcasing Sri Lanka's rich cultural heritage.
