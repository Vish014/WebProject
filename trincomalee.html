About Trincomalee City

Trincomalee, located on the northeastern coast of Sri Lanka, is a port city renowned for its beautiful beaches, natural deep-water harbor, and significant historical and cultural sites. It has been a major maritime trading port for centuries, influenced by various colonial powers.

The city offers a relaxed coastal atmosphere combined with opportunities for adventure and cultural exploration. Key highlights of Trincomalee include:

  • Nilaveli Beach and Uppuveli Beach, famous for their pristine sands and clear waters, ideal for swimming and sunbathing.
  • Pigeon Island National Park, a popular spot for snorkeling and diving, known for its coral reefs and diverse marine life.
  • Koneswaram Temple, an ancient Hindu temple perched on Swami Rock, offering stunning views of the ocean.
  • Fort Frederick, a historic fort built by the Portuguese, now housing the Koneswaram Temple and providing scenic walks.
  • Opportunities for whale and dolphin watching during the season (typically May to October).

Best Time to Visit

Peak Season

May to October

Shoulder Season

April, November

Good weather, fewer crowds, transition periods.

Off-Peak
