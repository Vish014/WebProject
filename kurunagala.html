About Kurunagala City

Kurunagala, located in the North Western Province, is a historic city that once served as a royal capital of Sri Lanka. It is distinctively characterized by its massive rock formations, which dominate the landscape and offer unique natural attractions.

The city combines historical significance with natural beauty, providing visitors with opportunities for exploration and relaxation. Key highlights of Kurunagala include:

  • Ethagala (Elephant Rock), a prominent rock formation with a large Buddha statue offering panoramic views.
  • Kurunagala Lake, a serene body of water ideal for leisurely walks and relaxation.
  • Ridi Viharaya (Silver Temple), an ancient Buddhist temple with intricate carvings and historical importance.
  • Proximity to other ancient sites like Panduwasnuwara, offering insights into early Sri Lankan kingdoms.
  • A blend of urban life with a backdrop of natural rock hills, providing a unique atmosphere.

Featured Activities

Climb Ethagala Rock

Hike up Ethagala (Elephant Rock) to admire the giant Buddha statue and enjoy panoramic views of the city.

Visit Ridi Viharaya

Explore the ancient Ridi Viharaya (Silver Temple), known for its historical significance and beautiful architecture.

Relax by Kurunagala Lake

Enjoy a peaceful stroll or boat ride on Kurunagala Lake, offering serene views and a refreshing atmosphere.
