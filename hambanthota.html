About Hambanthota City

Hambanthota is a significant city on the southeastern coast of Sri Lanka, rapidly emerging as a key economic and tourism hub. Known for its dry zone climate, expansive salt pans, and proximity to major wildlife parks, it offers a unique blend of natural beauty and developing infrastructure.

The city is strategically important with its deep-sea port and international airport, while also serving as a gateway to some of the island's most incredible wildlife experiences. Key highlights of Hambanthota include:

  • Proximity to Bundala National Park, a Ramsar wetland site famous for birdwatching and migratory birds.
  • Close access to Yala National Park, renowned for its high density of leopards and diverse wildlife.
  • The Mattala Rajapaksa International Airport and Hambanthota International Port, modern infrastructure projects.
  • Beautiful coastal stretches and traditional fishing villages.
  • The Dry Zone Botanical Garden, showcasing flora adapted to arid conditions.
